Suspension, Termination, and Enforcement

SilentFlare may suspend, restrict, rate-limit, remove, quarantine, or terminate access when a user violates these terms or creates legal, security, payment, sanctions, infrastructure, or abuse risk.

Examples

  • Abuse reports, malware, spam, phishing, illegal content, or network attacks.
  • False KYC, forged documents, identity mismatch, or regional restriction evasion.
  • Payment failure, chargeback, fraud warning, or unpaid invoice.
  • Requests from infrastructure providers, payment processors, courts, or competent authorities.

Evidence and cooperation

SilentFlare may preserve relevant logs, account records, billing records, and abuse evidence when needed to investigate incidents or comply with lawful obligations.
