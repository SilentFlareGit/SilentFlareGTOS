# Legal, Abuse, Privacy, and Contact Rules

## 1. Purpose

1.1 This policy explains how users and third parties should contact SilentFlare about blog issues, abuse reports, legal notices, privacy requests, crawler blocks, KYC1 review, security reports, comments, and restricted-region access.

1.2 Correct routing helps SilentFlare respond faster, reduce abuse, and preserve evidence properly.

## 2. General contact

2.1 General blog, correction, attribution, comment, link, crawler, or technical questions should be sent through the designated contact channel shown on the blog, repository, profile, or relevant page.

2.2 Requests should include relevant URLs, dates, screenshots if useful, logs if safe to share, IP addresses where relevant, user agents where relevant, and a clear description of the issue.

2.3 Users must not include passwords, private keys, seed phrases, full payment card numbers, unnecessary identity documents, or unnecessary sensitive data in ordinary messages.

## 3. Abuse reports

3.1 Abuse reports should include the affected URL, IP address, domain, timestamp with timezone, logs, headers, screenshots, comment identifier, crawler user agent, transaction identifier if any, or other evidence.

3.2 Reports involving malware, phishing, spam, DDoS, unauthorized access, copyright infringement, child safety, threats, fraud, unlawful content, personal data exposure, or malicious crawling should be clearly labeled.

3.3 SilentFlare may prioritize reports based on severity, evidence quality, legal risk, user risk, provider deadlines, and operational impact.

## 4. Security reports

4.1 Security researchers should report vulnerabilities responsibly and avoid destructive testing, data access, persistence, lateral movement, social engineering, spam, service degradation, or public disclosure before remediation.

4.2 Reports should include reproduction steps, impact, affected endpoint, proof of concept, and suggested remediation.

4.3 SilentFlare may restrict or deny testing that causes harm, violates law, exposes private data, or affects third parties.

## 5. Crawler, scraping, and block appeals

5.1 A crawler operator, mirror operator, archive operator, researcher, or user appealing a block should include the affected IP address or range, ASN if known, user agent, target URLs, request volume, purpose, contact address, and proposed rate limits.

5.2 SilentFlare may require traffic reduction, crawler identification, robots compliance, KYC1, restricted-region review, or written commitments before unblocking or allowlisting.

5.3 SilentFlare may deny appeals that are incomplete, misleading, abusive, repetitive, high-risk, or inconsistent with local internet-law compliance.

## 6. Legal notices

6.1 Legal notices should clearly identify the sender, legal basis, affected URL or content, requested action, evidence, jurisdiction, deadline, and contact details.

6.2 SilentFlare may require additional verification before acting on a legal notice.

6.3 SilentFlare may reject incomplete, abusive, fraudulent, unclear, overbroad, or legally defective notices.

## 7. Privacy and data-rights requests

7.1 Privacy requests should identify the relevant data and the specific right being exercised, such as access, correction, deletion, restriction, objection, or export.

7.2 SilentFlare may require identity verification, including KYC1 where appropriate, before fulfilling a privacy request.

7.3 SilentFlare may refuse or limit requests where permitted by law, including where data must be retained for abuse prevention, crawler-abuse prevention, fraud prevention, legal compliance, provider obligations, dispute resolution, or legal claims.

## 8. KYC1 submissions

8.1 Users should not send identity documents unless SilentFlare specifically requests KYC1 through an official review channel.

8.2 A KYC1 request should only ask for information reasonably necessary for anti-abuse, restricted-region, crawler, or regional-compliance review.

8.3 SilentFlare may delete, reject, or ignore unnecessary identity documents sent without request.

## 9. Communications from SilentFlare

9.1 SilentFlare may send notices by email, website notice, repository update, profile notice, page notice, feed notice, contact-channel reply, or other reasonable communication method.

9.2 The user is responsible for keeping contact information current and monitoring relevant communication channels when requesting review or allowlisting.

9.3 Notices are deemed received when sent, posted, or made available, unless mandatory law requires otherwise.

## 10. Abusive communications

10.1 Users must not threaten, harass, spam, dox, impersonate, blackmail, or abuse SilentFlare, users, providers, reporters, or third parties.

10.2 SilentFlare may limit contact, close messages, block users, remove comments, deny appeals, or refuse future interaction for abusive communications.

## 11. Emergency matters

11.1 Emergency reports involving imminent harm, active compromise, severe abuse, child safety, terrorism, credible threats, or major security incidents should be clearly marked as urgent and include immediately actionable evidence.

11.2 SilentFlare may take emergency action before completing full review where necessary to reduce harm.
