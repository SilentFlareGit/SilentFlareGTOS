# Enforcement, Suspension, and Termination

## 1. Enforcement purpose

1.1 SilentFlare enforces these terms to protect users, third parties, infrastructure, providers, legal compliance, payment systems, and service reputation.

1.2 Enforcement may be automated, manual, provider-driven, complaint-driven, authority-driven, or risk-based.

## 2. Investigation

2.1 SilentFlare may investigate abuse reports, payment disputes, provider complaints, security alerts, KYC inconsistencies, sanctions signals, restricted-region signals, traffic anomalies, and suspected violations.

2.2 During investigation, SilentFlare may review account data, logs, traffic metadata, payment metadata, KYC data, support communications, configuration data, and other relevant information.

2.3 SilentFlare may request information from the user. Failure to respond truthfully and promptly may result in suspension or termination.

## 3. Immediate action

3.1 SilentFlare may take immediate action without prior notice where necessary to stop harm, comply with law, satisfy provider requirements, prevent abuse, protect security, preserve evidence, or reduce risk.

3.2 Immediate action may include blocking traffic, disabling features, revoking API keys, disabling sessions, suspending accounts, freezing billing, restricting exports, removing content, or terminating service.

## 4. Notice

4.1 SilentFlare may provide notice by email, dashboard message, support channel, repository notice, website notice, or other reasonable method.

4.2 SilentFlare may withhold notice where notice would create risk, reveal detection methods, violate law, compromise an investigation, or enable further abuse.

## 5. Suspension

5.1 Suspension may be temporary or indefinite.

5.2 During suspension, the user may lose access to dashboards, APIs, content, exports, billing functions, support priority, or other features.

5.3 SilentFlare may require remediation, KYC, payment resolution, security fixes, abuse explanation, written commitments, or provider approval before reactivation.

## 6. Termination

6.1 SilentFlare may terminate service if the user violates these terms, fails KYC, creates unacceptable risk, engages in abuse, files abusive chargebacks, provides false information, violates law, or causes provider enforcement.

6.2 SilentFlare may terminate inactive, abandoned, unpaid, or obsolete accounts after reasonable notice where practical.

6.3 Termination may be permanent and may include refusal of future accounts.

## 7. Content removal and preservation

7.1 SilentFlare may remove, disable, quarantine, preserve, or restrict content where required by law, provider rules, abuse policy, or security needs.

7.2 SilentFlare may preserve evidence of abuse, fraud, payment disputes, KYC fraud, security incidents, or legal requests after account termination.

7.3 SilentFlare may deny export of content where export would violate law, harm third parties, compromise security, or facilitate abuse.

## 8. Refund impact

8.1 Suspension or termination for abuse, fraud, false KYC, sanctions risk, restricted-region circumvention, illegal activity, payment abuse, provider complaints, or serious policy violation may make the user ineligible for refund.

8.2 SilentFlare may offset unpaid amounts, provider costs, chargeback fees, damages, or investigation costs against any refund where permitted by law.

## 9. Appeals

9.1 The user may request review of an enforcement action by contacting the designated support or legal channel.

9.2 The appeal should include account identifier, relevant dates, explanation, evidence, remediation steps, and any documents requested by SilentFlare.

9.3 SilentFlare may deny appeals that are abusive, incomplete, misleading, repetitive, or high-risk.

## 10. Repeat abuse

10.1 SilentFlare may permanently block users, devices, payment methods, identity documents, IP ranges, domains, organizations, or related accounts associated with repeat abuse.

10.2 SilentFlare may treat related accounts as one account for enforcement where there is shared ownership, control, payment method, device, address, infrastructure, content, behavior, or evasion pattern.
