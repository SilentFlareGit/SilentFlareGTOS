## 1. Hosting location and compliance baseline

1.1 The SilentFlare blog may be hosted on servers, networks, DNS, CDN, repository, or infrastructure located in Germany or the European Union.

1.2 Because infrastructure may be located in Germany or the European Union, users must comply with German law, European Union law, applicable local law, hosting provider policies, network abuse rules, sanctions rules, export-control rules, and lawful requests from competent authorities.

1.3 A user may not claim that local law permits conduct that is illegal, restricted, abusive, or unsafe under German law, European Union law, provider rules, or these terms.

## 2. Compliance with local internet rules

2.1 The user is responsible for determining whether the blog may legally be accessed, read, indexed, quoted, archived, mirrored, commented on, scraped, or shared in the user's jurisdiction.

2.2 SilentFlare may restrict, block, rate-limit, or require KYC1 where access from a jurisdiction creates unreasonable legal, regulatory, security, sanctions, export-control, privacy, local internet-law, crawler-abuse, or provider-compliance risk.

2.3 SilentFlare may refuse access or keep blocks in place where the user cannot provide information reasonably required to assess regional or anti-abuse risk.

## 3. German and EU legal restrictions

3.1 The user must not use the blog, comments, feeds, contact channels, repositories, or links for activity prohibited by German or European Union law, including unlawful hate speech, extremist content, terrorist content, child sexual abuse material, unlawful harassment, fraud, malware, unauthorized access, identity theft, phishing, copyright infringement, sanctions evasion, or illegal financial activity.

3.2 The user must not publish, submit, transmit, link, mirror, index, archive, proxy, relay, or facilitate unlawful content or traffic through the blog.

3.3 The user must not use the blog to evade blocking, filtering, monitoring, licensing, age restriction, sanctions, export controls, court orders, administrative orders, or provider enforcement.

## 4. Sanctions and export control

4.1 The user represents that the user is not subject to sanctions, asset freezes, trade restrictions, export-control restrictions, or other prohibitions that would make blog access unlawful or high risk.

4.2 The user must not provide copied content, feeds, mirrors, credentials, internal URLs, crawler access, or derived access to any sanctioned person, entity, region, organization, or prohibited end user.

4.3 The user must not use the blog to support military end uses, prohibited surveillance, cyber abuse, state-sponsored attacks, sanctions evasion, or other restricted end uses.

4.4 SilentFlare may review IP addresses, metadata, traffic behavior, comments, contact messages, identity documents submitted for KYC1, and other signals for sanctions and export-control risk.

## 5. Anti-abuse and provider compliance

5.1 SilentFlare may be required to comply with upstream hosting, network, DNS, registrar, CDN, repository, email, analytics, anti-abuse, or security provider policies.

5.2 If an upstream provider reports abuse, requires blocking, requests verification, imposes traffic limits, or terminates infrastructure, SilentFlare may take immediate action affecting access without prior notice.

5.3 Users, crawler operators, and commenters must cooperate with abuse investigations and must provide accurate logs, user-agent details, IP details, contact details, remediation evidence, or KYC1 information when reasonably requested.

## 6. Lawful requests and notices

6.1 SilentFlare may respond to lawful requests from courts, regulators, police, data protection authorities, sanctions authorities, hosting providers, DNS providers, repository providers, CDN providers, or other competent bodies.

6.2 SilentFlare may preserve, disclose, restrict, block, or remove information where legally required or reasonably necessary to protect the blog, third parties, infrastructure, or SilentFlare.

6.3 SilentFlare may notify the affected user of a lawful request when legally permitted, operationally safe, and reasonably practical.

## 7. User warranties

7.1 The user warrants that any comment, contact message, KYC1 submission, abuse appeal, crawler identification, user-agent claim, attribution claim, or legal notice provided to SilentFlare is accurate, current, complete, and not misleading.

7.2 The user warrants that use of the blog will not expose SilentFlare to unlawful activity, provider policy violations, sanctions violations, privacy violations, copyright infringement, abuse complaints, or unreasonable operational risk.

## 8. Compliance review

8.1 SilentFlare may review traffic, comments, feeds, crawler behavior, contact messages, IP addresses, ASNs, geolocation signals, and KYC1 materials before or after granting access.

8.2 SilentFlare may require KYC1, request crawler identification, limit rates, disable comments, block feeds, block IP ranges, block ASNs, remove content, or deny allowlisting if compliance risk is unresolved.

8.3 Failure to complete a reasonable compliance request may result in continued blocking or restriction.

## 9. No circumvention

9.1 The user must not evade compliance controls using VPNs, proxies, false crawler identities, misleading user agents, borrowed documents, synthetic identities, mirrors, traffic relays, remote desktops, or automated rotation.

9.2 If SilentFlare detects circumvention, SilentFlare may block, rate-limit, remove, preserve evidence, deny access, and report the matter where required or appropriate.
